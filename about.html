About This Research

Information Aggregation in Decentralized Markets: A Risk Monitoring Framework for Economic Policy Uncertainty

Abstract

Effectively monitoring Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) is crucial for early risk sensing; however, traditional EPU indices, primarily news-based, are often limited by reporting latency and subjective bias. To address these limitations, this paper proposes Poly-EPU, a high-frequency monitoring framework that utilizes Polymarket transaction data to capture forward-looking crowd wisdom for uncertainty sensing.

Specifically, this framework first screens EPU-related markets by applying Large Language Model (LLM), then constructs the Poly-EPU index by information derived from market prices. Empirical results demonstrate that Poly-EPU: (1) accurately detects major risk events, validating its real-time sensing capabilities; (2) correlates significantly with traditional EPU indices, confirming its validity as a macro-information proxy; (3) exhibits long-term cointegration with the VIX, and reveals significant spillover effects that serve as robust early-warning signals.

Key Contributions

1

Innovative Data Source

Leverages prediction markets as a novel data source for EPU measurement, overcoming frequency and incentive constraints of traditional text analysis. Employs LLM for targeted market screening.

2

Novel Index Methodology

Proposes an index synthesis algorithm integrating static uncertainty (information entropy) and dynamic uncertainty (price volatility) with volume-weighted aggregation.

3

Validated Effectiveness

Systematically evaluates validity through ARDL modeling, demonstrating significant long-term positive spillover effect on VIX with robust leading predictive capacity.
