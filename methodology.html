Poly-EPU Methodology

Understanding how the Economic Policy Uncertainty Index is constructed from prediction market data

Overview

The Poly-EPU index is a novel Economic Policy Uncertainty (EPU) measurement framework that leverages transaction data from Polymarket, the world's leading decentralized prediction market platform. Unlike traditional EPU indices that rely on news text analysis (BBD-EPU), Poly-EPU captures forward-looking crowd wisdom through real capital-at-stake market transactions.

Key Advantages

  • High Frequency: Daily updates compared to monthly traditional indices
  • Forward-Looking: Captures market expectations, not historical events
  • Capital-Verified: Traders risk real money, filtering noise and bias
  • Real-Time: Immediate response to policy events and announcements

Market Selection

Markets are screened for EPU relevance using an LLM-based filtering system that evaluates market titles and descriptions against the 11 policy categories defined by Baker, Bloom, and Davis (2016), supplemented with policymaker changes.

Monetary Policy

Fed decisions, interest rates, inflation, Powell statements

Fiscal Policy

Budget, debt ceiling, government spending, infrastructure

Trade Policy

Tariffs, trade agreements, WTO, sanctions

Taxes

Tax reform, corporate tax, IRS policies

Health Care

Medicaid, FDA regulations, healthcare reform

National Security

Defense spending, military actions, geopolitics

Financial Regulation

SEC/CFTC decisions, banking supervision, crypto

Regulation (General)

Antitrust, immigration, EPA, labor laws

Entitlements

Social Security, unemployment, welfare programs

Sovereign Debt

Default risk, currency stability, debt markets

Elections

Presidential, congressional, gubernatorial races

Policymakers

Fed Chair nominations, cabinet appointments

Index Calculation

The Poly-EPU index combines two dimensions of uncertainty:

1. Static Uncertainty (Information Entropy)

Measures the equilibrium degree of probability distribution:

Entropyi,t = -Pi,t·log₂(Pi,t) - (1-Pi,t)·log₂(1-Pi,t)

When P = 0.5, entropy reaches maximum (=1), indicating highest uncertainty. When P approaches 0 or 1, entropy approaches 0 (certainty).

2. Dynamic Uncertainty (EWMA Volatility)

Captures abrupt changes in market beliefs:

Volatilityi,t = λ·Volatilityi,t-1 + (1-λ)·|ΔPi,t|

λ = 0.94 (RiskMetrics standard decay factor)
|ΔPi,t| = |Pi,t - Pi,t-1| (absolute daily price change)

3. Market Score

Combines static and dynamic uncertainty:

MarketScorei,t = α·Entropyi,t + (1-α)·Volatilityi,t

α = 0.5 (equal weight to both dimensions)

4. Volume-Weighted Aggregation

Final index weighted by trading volume:

Weighti,t = Volumei,t / Σj∈StVolumej,t
Poly-EPUt = Σi∈St Weighti,t·MarketScorei,t

Higher-volume markets receive greater weight, reflecting more aggregated information.

Validation

The Poly-EPU index has been validated through multiple empirical tests:

Event Detection

Accurately captures major policy events including U.S. presidential elections, Fed policy reviews, and geopolitical conflicts.

BBD-EPU Correlation

Monthly correlation coefficient of 0.8782 with the benchmark BBD-EPU index (p < 0.001), confirming validity as a macro information proxy.

VIX Spillover

Exhibits significant long-term cointegration with VIX. ARDL model shows positive spillover effect with ~8.5 day absorption half-life.

Leading Indicator

Demonstrates predictive power for market volatility, providing an early warning window for policy-induced market movements.

Model Parameters

Parameter Symbol Value Description
EWMA Decay Factor λ 0.94 RiskMetrics standard for daily financial data
Entropy-Volatility Weight α 0.50 Equal weight to static and dynamic uncertainty
Price Data Frequency fidelity 1440 Daily price observations (1440 minutes)
